## [OpenAI unveils first custom AI chip 'Jalapeño' with Broadcom](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

OpenAI announced its first custom AI inference chip, called Jalapeño, developed in collaboration with Broadcom and manufactured by TSMC, with development accelerated by OpenAI's own models. This marks a major step for OpenAI to reduce reliance on external chip suppliers and optimize cost and efficiency for running AI inference workloads, potentially influencing the AI hardware landscape. The chip is an application-specific integrated circuit (ASIC) optimized for inference, and its development from design to production took only nine months, partly accelerated by OpenAI's own AI models.

**Background**: AI inference chips are specialized processors designed to run trained AI models to make predictions, as opposed to training chips. ASICs are custom chips for a specific task, offering higher efficiency than general-purpose processors. By designing its own chip, OpenAI aims to lower inference costs and improve performance for its services like ChatGPT.

The blog notes that GitHub recently introduced configurable PR limits for maintainers, but some commenters argue that unlike email spam—where sender reputation is tied to IPs and domains—PR spam lacks analogous infrastructure for individual user reputation.

The analogy to email spam draws on the shared problem of scaling reputation and filtering mechanisms in open, decentralized platforms.

## [Generative AI may harm Chinese students' exam scores](https://cepr.org/publications/dp21577) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A 30-month study of 26,811 Chinese middle and high school students found that while generative AI improves homework scores by 18% and reduces completion time by 30%, it leads to a 20% drop in closed-book monthly exams and 18-24% decline in high-stakes tests like zhongkao and gaokao. This large-scale empirical study provides strong evidence that over-reliance on generative AI for homework can undermine genuine learning, with significant implications for education policy and AI regulation in schools worldwide. The negative effects were most pronounced in social sciences, followed by STEM and languages; low-grade, high-achieving, and male students were more affected. About 80% of AI users exhibited 'homework outsourcing' behavior—very short homework time but high scores—and these students bore most of the losses.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jun 24, 05:15

**Background**: Generative AI tools like ChatGPT can produce written assignments quickly, leading some students to use them as a shortcut. This study tracked students over 30 months to measure the long-term impact on learning outcomes. The findings highlight the risk of AI undermining foundational knowledge when used as a substitute for effort rather than a learning aid.
